# Negative Prompts — What to Avoid

These prompts and constraints prevent the most common errors when generating Mesoamerican-inspired outputs.

---

## Universal Anti-Patterns

- `"no tribal tattoo patterns"` — tribal tattoo aesthetics are a modern western stylization unrelated to any specific Mesoamerican tradition
- `"no aztec calendar cliché stamp"` — the Sun Stone is one specific late Aztec monument; avoid using it as a generic Mesoamerican symbol
- `"no euro-medieval ornament"` — acanthus leaves, baroque scrollwork, Gothic tracery, Celtic knotwork are European; none belong here
- `"no modern logo-style simplification"` — Mesoamerican symbols have internal complexity; oversimplified flat icons lose their semantic richness
- `"no symmetrical mandala format without cultural basis"` — not all Mesoamerican art is mandalic; many cultures use registers and narrative strips
- `"no text watermarks, no modern typography"` — modern typefaces break the visual system entirely
- `"no gradient shading in codex-style"` — flat fills with hard black outlines; codex painting has NO airbrushed gradients
- `"no floating unanchored elements"` — figures in register-band compositions stand on baselines; glyphs are in cartouche blocks; nothing floats randomly
- `"no random feather scatter"` — feathers are organized (radial fans, sequential bundles, structured plumes); never randomly scattered

---

## Culture-Specific Anti-Patterns

### Aztec
- `"no gradient or volumetric shading in codex figures"` — codex painting is flat
- `"no realistic portraiture in codex style"` — figures are symbolic, not individualistic or photorealistic
- `"no horror-movie skull treatment"` — skulls in Aztec art are integrated cosmological symbols, not horror decorations
- `"no sun/moon as generic cheerful icons"` — they carry specific symbolic weight; use culture-specific solar imagery

### Maya
- `"no empty background in Classic Maya compositions"` — must fill with waterlily scrolls or glyph blocks (horror vacui)
- `"no Maya Blue used casually"` — it is a precious, sacred pigment; should feel rare
- `"no profile figures in a full horror-vacui background"` — the background fill should never overwhelm and obscure the figure
- `"no generic hieroglyphics that don't follow Maya glyph block structure"` — random squiggles are not glyphs

### Olmec
- `"no flat 2D graphic codex-style treatment for Olmec subjects"` — Olmec is about sculptural 3D mass
- `"no bright polychrome colors for Olmec"` — Olmec palette is dark basalt + jade green + occasional cinnabar
- `"no complex surface pattern filling"` — Olmec surfaces are smooth; detail is minimal and concentrated in facial features

### Teotihuacan
- `"no diagonal composition in mural-style"` — strict horizontal registers only
- `"no profile figure orientation"` — Teotihuacan figures are frontally oriented
- `"no individual portraiture"` — Teotihuacan art is systematic/modular, not individualistic
- `"no empty background without filler symbols"` — water drops, shells, small scrolls must fill background in mural style

### Zapotec
- `"no dynamic or action poses for Zapotec figures"` — strictly frontal, static, symmetrical
- `"no colorful polychrome on Zapotec urns"` — grey-buff clay is the natural surface; minimal color

### Mixtec
- `"no horror vacui background fill"` — Mixtec codices have clean backgrounds with sparse labels
- `"no shading or volume"` — flat articulated figures with precise costumes only

### Toltec
- `"no symbolic density/complexity in individual figures"` — Toltec figures are standardized and simple; depth is through repetition
- `"no curved or flowing line quality"` — Toltec is stiffer and more angular than Maya

### Veracruz / Gulf Coast
- `"no angular step-fret as the primary motif"` — in Veracruz, the scroll/S-curve is primary; step-fret is secondary
- `"no solemn or austere tone for Veracruz smiling figurines"` — they are joyful, playful, festive

## Cross-Cultural Confusion Anti-Patterns

- `"no Maya glyph blocks on Aztec codex figures"` — Maya and Aztec writing are distinct
- `"no Aztec flat-codex style applied to Olmec sculpture"` — Olmec is sculptural mass, not graphic flatness
- `"no goggle eyes (Tlaloc) on Maya Chaac figures"` — Maya Chaac has a hook/scroll nose, not goggle eyes
- `"no interlocking scroll pattern labeled as Aztec"` — the interlocking scroll is specifically Veracruz/Tajín
- `"no Zapotec frontal urns labeled as Maya pottery"` — Zapotec grey-buff urns are distinct from Maya polychrome
- `"no Aztec day signs in a Maya context without specifying hybridity"` — day sign glyphs are culturally distinct
