# Aztec (Mexica) Visual Culture Reference

**Period:** ~1300–1521 CE  
**Region:** Central Mexico, Basin of Mexico, Tenochtitlan (modern Mexico City)  
**Also known as:** Mexica (the people), Nahua, Triple Alliance

---

## Overview for Generative AI

The Aztec visual system is one of the most codified and symbol-dense in Mesoamerica. It synthesizes influences from Teotihuacan, Toltec, and other earlier cultures into a unified imperial aesthetic. Key qualities: aggressive geometry, interlocking symbolic systems, high-contrast black-and-red codex tradition, and monumental stone sculpture.

---

## Core Visual Characteristics

- **High-contrast black outline with flat color fills** — codex illustrations use strong black contours enclosing flat zones of red, turquoise, yellow, white
- **Symbolic density** — figures carry multiple layered attributes (headdress type, body paint, implements, glyphs) that encode identity and status
- **Frontal/profile duality** — figures shown in profile but with frontal torso; eyes frontally placed in profile heads
- **Feather work dominance** — elaborate feathered headdresses, shields (chimalli), and cloaks are primary status markers
- **Stone monumental sculpture** — massive, blocky, heavily stylized forms; Coatlicue, the Sun Stone, Coyolxauhqui disk
- **Skull imagery** — skulls integrated naturalistically into ornament (Mictlantecuhtli, Tzompantli)
- **Eagle and jaguar** — warrior society symbols; eagle for solar day warriors, jaguar for nocturnal/earth warriors

---

## Key Motifs

### Geometric / Structural
- **Xicalcoliuhqui** — stepped-spiral or scrolled fret; the defining Aztec geometric border; right-angle scrolled meander
- **Chalchihuitl** — jade/precious stone symbol; a circle with four outer dots and a cross motif; signals preciousness and water
- **Tlaltecuhtli** — earth-monster crouch; massive crouching toad-like figure with clawed joints; used on bases of monuments
- **Tzompantli rows** — repeated skull faces in horizontal banded registers
- **4-petal flower** — solar/fertility symbol; Nahuatl: xochitl; occurs in borders and floor inlays

### Cosmological
- **Ollin** — movement/earthquake glyph; four directional arms meeting a central point; used in the Sun Stone center
- **Nahui Ollin (4-Movement)** — the fifth sun's name glyph; appears as the current cosmic era symbol
- **Cardinal directions** — color-coded: East = red/yellow, North = black, West = white, South = blue/green
- **Tonatiuh disk** — solar face motif; radiating sun-face with extended tongue; geometric ray bands
- **Cipactli** — primordial crocodile; earth monster; spiny irregular silhouette

### Figurative
- **Eagle warrior costume** — full eagle-head helmet, feathered suit, clawed gloves/boots
- **Jaguar warrior costume** — jaguar skin suit, open jaguar-head helmet
- **Quetzalcoatl** — feathered serpent; serpent body with quetzal plumage at head and rattle
- **Tlaloc mask** — goggle eyes, fanged jaw, serpentine upper lip; rain deity face
- **Coatlicue skirt** — skirt of interlocked serpents; head replaced by two serpent heads facing each other to form a face

## Writing and Glyph System

- **Type:** Logograms + phonetic complements + pictographic narrative (not fully phonetic)
- **Codices:** Codex Borgia (ritual), Codex Mendoza (tribute/history), Codex Tonalamatl
- **Glyph block structure:** Each name/concept is a compact pictographic unit, often with phonetic qualifiers attached (feet for motion, footprints for path/journey)
- **Day signs (tonalpohualli):** 20 named days × 13 number coefficients = 260-day cycle; each day has a pictographic glyph
- **Year signs:** Reed, Flint, House, Rabbit — in combination with numbers 1–13
- **Speech scrolls:** Volute emerging from mouth = speech; larger volutes = song/chant
- **Footprint paths:** Row of footprints = journey; used in narrative codices

## Calendars

### Tonalpohualli (260-day sacred calendar)
- 20 day signs × 13 number coefficients
- Used for divination, naming, ritual scheduling
- Each 13-day period (trecena) governed by a patron deity
- Visually: a grid or wheel; day signs arrayed around the perimeter

### Xiuhpohualli (365-day solar calendar)
- 18 months of 20 days + 5 "nameless" days (Nemontemi)
- Month names correspond to agricultural and ritual cycles
- Visually: banded registers, one per month

### Calendar Round (52-year cycle)
- When Tonalpohualli and Xiuhpohualli align; celebrated with New Fire ceremony
- Visually represented as interlocked wheel diagrams

### Sun Stone (Sunstone / Aztec Calendar Stone)
- Not a functional calendar — a cosmological monument
- Center: Tonatiuh face (sun god), surrounded by 4 previous suns in cartouches
- Ring 2: 20 day signs
- Ring 3: turquoise sky band with star eyes
- Outer ring: two xiuhcoatl (fire serpents) meeting at the base
- **Generative note:** Concentric ring layout; each ring has its own tile density and motif type

## Architecture and Pattern

- **Stepped pyramid (teocalli):** Dual stairways on west face; paired shrines at summit; each addition buries previous structure
- **Talud-tablero:** Inherited from Teotihuacan; alternating sloped and vertical wall panels
- **Serpent balustrades:** Feathered serpent heads at base of stairways; undulating serpent-body balustrade
- **Tzompantli platform:** Carved skull racks framing the skull trophy wall
- **Floor mosaics:** Geometric step-fret inlays in plaza floors
- **Border bands:** Step-fret meander; xicalcoliuhqui scroll; running water-scroll bands

---

## Sculpture Typology

- **Colossal cult images:** Coatlicue (earth-death goddess); Tlaltecuhtli (earth monster); Tizoc Stone
- **Sacrificial vessels:** Cuauhxicalli (eagle vessel); circular stone bowls; heart-shaped depressions
- **Coyolxauhqui disk:** Dismembered moon goddess; radial composition; high-relief on circular format
- **Stelae and commemorative monuments:** Tizoc Stone records military conquests with procession of captive figures
- **Atlantean figures:** Upright warrior columns supporting altars

---

## Textile and Body Art

- **Tilmatli (cloak) patterns:** Geometric borders; checker, wave, and step-fret
- **Body paint:** Ritual body paint color-codes deity impersonators; black for priests/death, red for warriors, blue for sacrificial victims
- **Feather mosaic shields:** Geometric patterns in exotic feather work on wicker frames
- **Lip plugs, ear spools, nose ornaments:** Depicted consistently with culture-specific shape (jade bar lip plug, disk ear spool)

## Generative AI Directives

- Use **xicalcoliuhqui scroll** as a border element instead of generic meanders
- Codex-style means: **black outline dominant**, flat fill, no shading gradients
- For sculpture: **chunky, heavily undercut relief**, stone texture prominent, pigment in deepest grooves
- Feather work reads as **radial tapered strokes** in tight clusters; use quetzal green + iridescent shimmer
- Skull motifs should integrate into patterns **rhythmically, not as horror elements**
- Solar disk compositions: **always concentric rings** with distinct motif per ring
- Day signs can tile as a 4×5 or 5×4 grid (20 signs)
