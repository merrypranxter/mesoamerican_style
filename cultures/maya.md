# Maya Visual Culture Reference

**Period:** Pre-Classic (~2000 BCE), Classic (250–900 CE), Post-Classic (900–1521 CE)  
**Region:** Yucatan Peninsula, Chiapas, Guatemala, Belize, western Honduras  
**Languages/Scripts:** Ch'olti'an (Classic), Yucatec, K'iche', and others (~30 languages)

---

## Overview for Generative AI

The Maya visual tradition is among the most complex and refined in the ancient world. Classic Maya art is characterized by elegant figural sculpture, fully phonetic hieroglyphic writing, elaborate polychrome ceramic painting, and astronomical precision. The style shifts notably across periods and regions — from dense Classic lowland figural stelae to geometrically abstract Post-Classic Yucatan Puuc architecture.

---

## Core Visual Characteristics

- **Flowing, curvilinear forms** — figures rendered with elegant contour lines; bodies show slight torsion and foreshortening unlike the flat Aztec profile
- **Horror vacui in Classic period** — background space densely filled with secondary glyphs, scrolls, and decorative elements
- **Double-outline technique** — forms bounded by both a thin inner line and a heavier outer contour
- **Waterlily / scroll infill** — decorative fill scrolls shaped like waterlily pads and curled volutes; fills empty space
- **Witz (mountain) monster** — animate mountain face at doorway thresholds; open mouth as entrance
- **Celts and regalia** — jade celts (axe-form objects) carried or worn as elite symbols
- **Elongated skull beauty ideal** — elite heads shown with sloped/flattened foreheads (achieved by board-binding in infancy)
- **Jester God diadem** — trilobed headband with central "jester" element; marks kings
- **Serpent Bar** — double-headed sky-serpent held horizontally by rulers in period portraits (Palenque, Tikal)

## Key Motifs

### Geometric / Structural
- **Mat pattern (pop)** — interlaced diagonal grid like woven mat; symbol of rulership (pop = mat = throne)
- **Stepped platform silhouette** — stacked trapezoidal steps; appears in glyph compounds and architectural profiles
- **Kan cross** — four-directional cross with loops; water, preciousness, centrality
- **Quatrefoil** — four-petal opening in the earth; portal to underworld or cave; appears in stelae bases
- **Double-spiral scroll** — volute pair; stands for breath, wind, speech
- **Crosshatching** — diagonal fill indicating obsidian, darkness, or preciousness in glyph contexts
- **Step-fret meander** — geometric Greek-key-like border; common in Puuc architecture frieze bands

### Cosmological / Symbolic
- **World Tree (Wacah Chan)** — axis mundi; a cross with foliated branches; portrayed on Palenque sarcophagus lid
- **Cauac sky band** — celestial band with symbols: kin (sun), akbal (night), imix (water), star cartouche; used as horizontal framing band
- **Serpent Vision** — deity or ancestor appearing in the open mouth of a rearing serpent (bloodletting ritual context)
- **Underworld road** — black road with bones; shown as dotted band through dark space
- **Maize god head** — elongated foliated head; represents maize, creation, rebirth; appears in Palenque, Copan
- **K'awiil (god K)** — lightning deity; smoking forehead, serpent-foot; appears in royal scepters

### Figurative
- **Ahau (lord) face glyph** — most common personification prefix; round-faced head with cruller under eye
- **Death head** — fleshless skull with spot markings; percentage-infill cheek
- **Jaguar God of the Underworld** — spotted jaguar face with cruller under eye; night sun in underworld
- **Chac mask** — hook-nosed rain deity; stacked Chac masks as architectural column decorations (Puuc style)
- **Itzamna** — creator deity; elderly lord; iguana-headed; associated with writing and sky

## Writing and Glyph System

- **Type:** Full writing system — logographic + syllabic (CV syllabary of ~100 signs); can write anything in the language
- **Glyph block structure:** Glyphs organized in paired columns read left-to-right, top-to-bottom; each glyph block can combine a main sign with affixes (prefix at top-left, postfix at bottom-right, infixed signs inside)
- **Main sign types:** Head variants (deity heads for numbers/days), full-figure variants (full body in a glyph space), geometric/abstract signs
- **Positional affixes:** Small elements that modify meaning — dots (phonetic) attached to main sign; bar (line) = 5 in numbers
- **Numbers:** Dots = 1, bars = 5, shell = zero; vigesimal (base-20) system
- **Coloring:** Classic carved glyphs are incised; painted glyphs use black outlines on cream; numbers sometimes color-coded

### Major Glyph Categories
- **Distance numbers** — Long Count date notation: Bak'tun, K'atun, Tun, Winal, K'in
- **Calendar Round** — Tzolk'in + Haab' combinations
- **Dedicatory formula** — standard sequence: date, event, name, title, parentage
- **Emblem glyphs** — city-state identification; each major center had its own emblem
- **Relationship glyphs** — u-k'ab ("it is the work of"), chum-wan ("is seated/installed"), hul ("arrives")

## Calendars

### Tzolk'in (260-day sacred calendar)
- 20 day names × 13 number coefficients = 260 unique day positions
- 20 day names: Imix, Ik', Ak'bal, K'an, Chikchan, Kimi, Manik', Lamat, Muluk, Ok, Chuwen, Eb', Ben, Ix, Men, Kib', Kaban, Etz'nab', Kawak, Ahaw
- **Visual:** Each day name is a distinct glyph block with characteristic internal geometry

### Haab' (365-day solar calendar)
- 18 months × 20 days + 5 unlucky days (Wayeb')
- Month glyphs: Pop, Wo, Sip, Sotz', Sek, Xul, Yaxk'in, Mol, Ch'en, Yax, Sak, Keh, Mak, K'ank'in, Muwan, Pax, K'ayab', Kumk'u, Wayeb'
- **Visual:** Each month is a distinct glyph; stacked registers of 18 month glyphs form a calendar band

### Long Count
- Absolute date system counting from mythological creation date (Aug 11, 3114 BCE in Gregorian)
- Format: 9.17.0.0.0 = Bak'tun . K'atun . Tun . Winal . K'in
- Typically written as 5 stacked glyph blocks with coefficients (dot-bar numbers)
- **Visual:** Vertical column of 5 date cartouches; each with a period glyph and coefficient

## Architecture and Pattern

### Classic Southern Lowlands (Palenque, Tikal, Copan)
- **Corbeled arch (false arch):** Inwardly stepped stone courses forming a triangular vault; creates tall narrow interior spaces
- **Roof comb:** Tall pierced stone latticework above temple; increases visual height; decorated with stucco sculpture
- **Frieze zone:** Horizontal band between body and roof; primary zone for stucco sculptural relief
- **Stairway text:** Stairway blocks each carved with a glyph — together tell a historical narrative (Copan Hieroglyphic Stairway)
- **Stelae in plaza:** Upright carved stone slabs recording rulers; paired with altars; arranged in rows facing temples

### Puuc Style (Uxmal, Kabah, Labna — 700–1000 CE)
- **Smooth lower zone:** Undecorated dressed-stone lower walls
- **Decorated upper frieze:** Intricate mosaic-like stone mosaic of geometric and deity masks
- **Stacked Chac masks:** Rain deity hook-nosed masks stacked vertically at corners; creates repetitive column of masks
- **Lattice/fretwork:** Geometric stone mosaic in the upper frieze — step-frets, lattices, key patterns
- **Columns:** Free-standing and engaged cylindrical columns; sometimes with stylized serpent form

### Post-Classic / Terminal Classic (Chichen Itza, Tulum)
- **Open gallery halls:** Colonnaded halls; multiple parallel stone columns
- **Toltec-Maya hybrid:** Warrior procession columns; chac mool altars; atlantean figures (Toltec influence)
- **Serpent columns:** Feathered serpent head at base, tail supporting the roof lintel above; giant coiling form
- **Descending god niche:** Inverted diving figure in niches (Tulum); diving deity in upside-down orientation

### Decorative System
- **Border band hierarchy:** Outermost = celestial band (cauac sky band); inner = day-sign glyphs; innermost = figural scene
- **Register stacking:** Multiple horizontal bands; each register spatially and narratively distinct
- **Cartouche frame:** Individual glyph blocks have rounded rectangular frames; stele compositions framed by glyph borders

## Ceramics

- **Classic Polychrome (Codex-style ceramics):** Black-and-red on cream; scenes of underworld lords, ball game, maize god; thin-walled, elegant
- **Palace-style pots:** Orange-on-cream with wide decorative frieze; mythological narrative scenes
- **Chama polychrome:** Rich polychrome with procession scenes; warm orange, red, black
- **Cylinder vases (K-vessels):** Cylindrical form; "Primary Standard Sequence" glyph band around rim; scene panel below
- **Plumed Serpent vessels:** Sculptural zoomorphic forms
- **Incensario (censer) stands:** Stacked flanged disks; deity mask on front; funerary/ritual use

---

## Sculpture and Relief

- **Stelae:** 2–9 m upright monoliths; front shows ruler in full regalia; back and sides have glyph texts; erected on Period-Ending dates
- **Lintels:** Carved stone beams above doorways; often finest relief carving; Yaxchilan lintels are masterworks
- **Altars:** Low circular or rectangular platforms; often paired with stelae; carved with captives, ritual scenes
- **Panels:** Rectangular carved stone panels (Palenque Palace Tablet, Temple of Inscriptions); detailed dynastic text+image
- **Stucco relief:** Modeled and painted stucco on facades and roof combs; highly three-dimensional

---

## Stylistic Distinguishing Features

- **Figural elegance:** Slim, elongated bodies with natural posture; rulers shown in 3/4 view
- **Horror vacui Classic filling:** Every space filled with secondary glyph or scroll motif
- **Waterlily scroll:** Distinctive curvilinear infill motif; found nowhere else in this exact form
- **Chac hook nose:** Architectural and figurative; instantly recognizable hook/scroll nose
- **Maya blue:** Color found nowhere else; vivid, permanent, sacred-quality blue
- **Corbeled vault silhouette:** Produces distinctive tall triangular interior space visible in cutaway diagrams

## Generative AI Directives

- Maya line quality is **elegant and curvilinear**, never harsh/angular like Aztec codex outlines
- Classic period: always fill background space with **waterlily scrolls or glyph blocks** (horror vacui)
- Use **Maya Blue** (#1B6BA8) as a key sacred accent; it should feel rare and special
- Glyph blocks are **paired columns**, read L→R, T→B; each block is approximately square
- Stelae compositions: tall vertical format; ruler figure in center; glyph border on all four sides; glyph columns flanking center
- Puuc architectural pattern: **lower half plain; upper half densely carved geometric mosaic**; corner columns of stacked masks
- Corbeled arch interior: tall pointed vault; smooth walls; colored with painted murals
- Long Count dates read as 5-glyph vertical stacks; use as decorative column element
